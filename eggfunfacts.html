Egg Facts Why did the egg get thrown out of class? Because it kept cracking jokes!
Chickens sometimes lay eggs with double yolks—it’s like finding a “twin” surprise inside!
An egg’s shell can have as many as 17,000 tiny pores—that’s a lot of “holes” for something so egg-straordinary!
The oldest recorded egg-laying chicken lived to be 16 years old—now that’s one “egg-sperienced” hen!
Some hens lay eggs that are so big, they don’t even fit in standard egg cartons—talk about an egg-cellent overachiever!
In some countries, people have egg-rolling contests—no yolk!
Did you know hummingbirds lay the smallest eggs, while ostriches lay the biggest? It’s a real case of egg-stremes!
If you drop an egg from a great height, it will egg-splode—so handle with care!
The world record for balancing eggs on end is 888 eggs at once—that’s an “egg-ceptional” feat!
Why did the egg sit on the computer? To hatch a new idea!
